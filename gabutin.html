Jacky’s Autobiography: A Story of Strength, Love, and Hope

“Even when life takes something away, love gives you strength to rise again.”

Hi there! I’m Jackelyn but you can call me Jacky, and I’m so glad you’re here. This website tells my story — one of love, loss, resilience, and hope. My journey hasn’t been easy, but every chapter has taught me the true meaning of strength and family.

The Beginning of My Journey

Many people don’t know that my story began with both joy and heartbreak. My mom passed away on the very evening of my 2nd birthday — a day that was meant to celebrate life became one that changed mine forever. Growing up without my mom was never easy, but I was blessed with a loving, supportive family who became my strength.

They taught me that even in pain, there’s purpose. Even in loss, there’s love. I learned that no matter how dark life gets, there’s always light waiting to shine again and I choose to keep shining for my mom and for everyone who believes in me.

“Life didn’t start easy, but it taught me to never give up.”

Hello Everyone, I’m Jackelyn Marie

Hello everyone! My name is Jackelyn Marie B. Gabutin, a 18 years old and a BIT student at Lapu-Lapu City College. I’m a dreamer, a believer, and someone who never gives up easily. I grew up learning how important kindness, family, and faith are in shaping who we become.

I love meeting new people, learning new things, and turning challenges into opportunities. Whether it’s helping others, creating something meaningful, or just spreading positivity, I always aim to inspire others through my story. One of my weaknesses is that when I make promises or plans with others and can’t fulfill them, I tend to feel they might be upset with me and I also feel shy or nervous when presenting or speaking in front of others. But I’m continuously working on these areas to build more confidence and courage to overcome them.

What I Love and What I Do

Lessons I’ve Learned

Life taught me that everything happens for a reason sometimes the pain we experience is preparing us for the purpose we’re meant to fulfill.

  • Family isn’t just who you’re born with — it’s who stands by you.
  • Strength is not about never falling, but about always getting back up.
  • Hope is powerful. Even when everything seems impossible, hold on.
